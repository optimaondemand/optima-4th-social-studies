Section A
Knowledge Check
⏲ ~10 min
1The Adams-Onis Treaty of 1819 resulted in which major change for Florida?
  • A) Florida became an independent country
  • B) Spain gave Florida to the United States
  • C) Florida was divided between France and England
  • D) The Seminole people gained control of Florida
Correct Answer: B
The Adams-Onis Treaty (also called the Transcontinental Treaty) was an agreement between Spain and the United States in which Spain ceded Florida to the U.S. in 1819. This ended centuries of Spanish control and made Florida a U.S. territory.
Common Misconceptions
Students may confuse "ceded" with "independence" (A) or mix up the European powers involved (C). Some may recall the Seminole Wars and incorrectly associate the treaty with Native American control (D).
2Which of these events happened FIRST in Florida's history?
  • A) Florida became a U.S. state in 1845
  • B) Fort Mose was established as a free Black settlement
  • C) The Civil War began
  • D) The Adams-Onis Treaty was signed
Correct Answer: B
Fort Mose was established in 1738 near St. Augustine, making it the first legally sanctioned free Black settlement in what is now the United States. This predates the Adams-Onis Treaty (1819), statehood (1845), and the Civil War (1861).
Common Misconceptions
Students often struggle with chronology. Some may pick the Adams-Onis Treaty (D) because it sounds "early" or choose statehood (A) because it is a founding event. Remind students to use their timeline knowledge: Fort Mose (1738) is the earliest.
3What was one major reason Florida's territorial leaders wanted statehood?
  • A) They wanted to end all farming in the territory
  • B) They wanted more say in the U.S. government and the ability to make their own laws
  • C) They hoped to return Florida to Spanish control
  • D) They wanted to move the capital to Miami
Correct Answer: B
As a territory, Florida had limited representation in Congress and could not fully govern itself. Territorial leaders wanted statehood so they could elect their own representatives, pass their own laws, and have a stronger voice in the federal government.
Common Misconceptions
Students may not understand the difference between a territory and a state. Some may pick absurd distractors (A, D) if they are guessing, which signals they did not retain the lesson content on statehood motivations.
4During the antebellum period, Florida's economy depended heavily on:
  • A) Tourism and space travel
  • B) Plantation agriculture and enslaved labor
  • C) Fishing and shipbuilding only
  • D) Gold mining and fur trading
Correct Answer: B
The antebellum period (before the Civil War) in Florida was dominated by plantation agriculture, especially cotton and sugar, which depended on the labor of enslaved people. This economic system was central to Florida's entry into the Confederacy.
Common Misconceptions
Students may associate Florida with tourism (A), which is a modern industry. Some may pick fishing (C) because Florida is surrounded by water, but this was not the dominant economic driver during the antebellum period.
5What did Reconstruction aim to do in Florida after the Civil War?
  • A) Rebuild the state and protect the rights of formerly enslaved people
  • B) Return Florida to Confederate control
  • C) Move all Floridians to northern states
  • D) Close all schools and churches
Correct Answer: A
Reconstruction was the period after the Civil War during which the federal government worked to rebuild Southern states and establish civil rights for formerly enslaved people, including the right to vote, attend school, and hold office.
Common Misconceptions
Students may confuse Reconstruction goals with pre-war conditions (B) or not understand that Reconstruction was about rebuilding and rights. If students pick B, they may be confusing Reconstruction with the "Redemption" era that followed it.
6Which is the correct order of these Florida events?
  • A) Civil War → Fort Mose → Statehood → Reconstruction
  • B) Fort Mose → Adams-Onis Treaty → Statehood → Civil War → Reconstruction
  • C) Statehood → Fort Mose → Civil War → Adams-Onis Treaty
  • D) Adams-Onis Treaty → Reconstruction → Fort Mose → Civil War
Correct Answer: B
The correct chronological order is: Fort Mose (1738) → Adams-Onis Treaty (1819) → Florida Statehood (1845) → Civil War (1861–1865) → Reconstruction (1865–1877). This is the core timeline students should have mastered across Units 7–8.
Common Misconceptions
This question tests overall chronological understanding. Students who select A or C are placing Fort Mose too late in the timeline. Students who select D may be confusing the order of Reconstruction and earlier events.

Historical Source: Florida Statehood (1845)

Read the following excerpt from a historical account describing Florida's transition from territory to state.

"When Florida became the 27th state on March 3, 1845, many settlers celebrated. They believed statehood would bring new roads, more protection from conflicts, and a stronger voice in Congress. But not everyone shared this joy. Enslaved people, who made up nearly half the population in some counties, had no voice in this decision. For them, statehood meant that the system of slavery would continue under new laws."
7Use the source above to answer the following question.
What does this source tell us about who benefited from Florida's statehood and who did not? Use details from the text.
Model Response (Secure — 3)
The source tells us that settlers benefited from statehood because they believed it would bring new roads, protection, and a stronger voice in Congress. However, enslaved people did not benefit. The source says they "had no voice in this decision" and that statehood meant slavery would continue under new laws. So the same event was good for some people but harmful for others.
Scoring Notes
Secure (3): Identifies both groups (settlers and enslaved people), uses specific text details (roads, protection, Congress, no voice, slavery continued). Developing (2): Mentions both groups but is vague or uses only one detail. Beginning (1): Names only one group or does not reference the source text.
8Think about what you have learned in Units 7–8 along with this source.
Based on this source and what you learned in Units 7–8, why might different groups of people have had very different feelings about the same event?
Model Response (Secure — 3)
Different groups had different feelings because statehood affected them in different ways. White settlers gained political power and hoped for economic growth, like new roads and trade. But enslaved people had no rights under the new state government, and statehood made it even harder for them to become free because Florida's laws protected slavery. From Units 7–8, we know that Florida's economy depended on enslaved labor, so the people in power wanted to keep that system going. This shows that the same event can be seen as positive or negative depending on a person's situation.
Scoring Notes
Secure (3): Connects the source to prior learning, explains how perspective depends on one's position in society, and references specific content from Units 7–8. Developing (2): Mentions different perspectives but does not clearly connect to Units 7–8 content. Beginning (1): Gives a one-sentence answer without evidence or connection to prior learning.

Socratic Response
⏲ ~10 min
🤔

This question asks students to make a claim and support it with evidence. Look for a clear claim statement and at least two supporting details.

9Read the prompt below.
Think about the events from Fort Mose through Reconstruction. Which ONE event do you believe most changed Florida? State your claim and give at least two pieces of evidence to support it.
Model Response (Secure — 3)
I believe that the Adams-Onis Treaty most changed Florida because it shifted control of the territory from Spain to the United States, which set off a chain of events that shaped everything that followed. First, the treaty made Florida a U.S. territory, which brought American settlers and laws and eventually led to statehood in 1845. Second, under U.S. control, the plantation system and slavery expanded rapidly, which changed Florida's economy and population. Without the Adams-Onis Treaty, Florida might have remained under Spanish rule, and the path to the Civil War and Reconstruction would have looked very different.
Scoring Notes
Accept any event from the Fort Mose–Reconstruction timeline as long as the student provides a clear claim and at least two pieces of evidence. Strong answers connect the chosen event to later consequences. Secure (3): Clear claim, 2+ specific evidence pieces, explains cause→effect. Developing (2): Has a claim and 1–2 evidence pieces but reasoning is incomplete. Beginning (1): No clear claim, or evidence is missing/irrelevant. Other valid claims: Florida statehood (1845), the Civil War, Fort Mose, Reconstruction — all are acceptable with proper evidence.

Virtue Lens Reflection
⏲ ~5 min
⚖️ Virtue Focus: Justice
Justice means treating people fairly and standing up for what is right, even when it is difficult.
10Reflect on what you learned in Units 7–8.
During Reconstruction, leaders tried to create fairer laws for all Floridians. Think of one example from Units 7–8 where someone showed the virtue of justice — treating people fairly even when it was hard. Who was it, what did they do, and why does it take courage to be just?
Model Response (Secure — 3)
One example of justice from Units 7–8 is the people who established Fort Mose. The Spanish governor of Florida allowed enslaved people who escaped from British colonies to live as free people at Fort Mose. This showed justice because he treated them as people who deserved freedom, even though slavery was common at the time. It took courage because it went against what many powerful people believed and could have caused conflict with the British colonies. Another example could be Reconstruction leaders who passed new laws giving formerly enslaved people the right to vote and go to school, even though many white Southerners opposed these changes.
Scoring Notes
Secure (3): Names a specific person or group, describes what they did, connects it to the virtue of justice, and explains why it required courage. Developing (2): Names an example and connects to justice but explanation of courage is vague. Beginning (1): No specific example, or no connection to justice. Accept any valid example from Units 7–8 content: Fort Mose founders, Reconstruction-era legislators, abolitionists, freedmen who built schools, etc.
