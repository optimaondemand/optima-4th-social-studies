Section A
Multiple Choice — Knowledge & Recall
1What was Fort Mose, and why was it historically significant?
  • A) A Spanish mission for converting Native peoples
  • B) The first legally sanctioned free Black settlement in what is now the United States, established in 1738
  • C) A British trading post in South Florida
  • D) A French fort on the Gulf Coast
✓ Correct Answer
B — The first legally sanctioned free Black settlement in what is now the United States, established in 1738
📝 Scoring Note
Students should recognize Fort Mose's unique significance as the first legally sanctioned free Black settlement.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may confuse Fort Mose with Spanish missions. Those who choose C or D may not yet distinguish between the different European powers' settlements in Florida.
2How many times did Florida change hands between European powers before becoming a U.S. territory?
  • A) Once
  • B) Twice
  • C) Three times — Spain to Britain to Spain to the U.S.
  • D) It was always controlled by Spain
✓ Correct Answer
C — Three times — Spain to Britain to Spain to the U.S.
📝 Scoring Note
Tests understanding of the sequence of colonial control over Florida — a key timeline concept.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose D may not have grasped that Florida was controlled by multiple European powers. Those who choose A or B may remember only part of the sequence.
3What was the Adams-Onis Treaty of 1819?
  • A) A peace treaty ending the Civil War
  • B) An agreement where Spain gave Florida to the United States
  • C) A trade deal between France and Britain
  • D) A law that created the state of Florida
✓ Correct Answer
B — An agreement where Spain gave Florida to the United States
📝 Scoring Note
Assesses knowledge of the specific treaty that transferred Florida to U.S. control.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may be confusing historical eras. Those who choose D may confuse territory status with statehood — Florida did not become a state until 1845.
4Why did enslaved people from British colonies escape to Spanish Florida?
  • A) Spain had better weather
  • B) Spain offered freedom to enslaved people who converted to Catholicism and pledged loyalty
  • C) There were no laws in Florida
  • D) Britain told them to leave
✓ Correct Answer
B — Spain offered freedom to enslaved people who converted to Catholicism and pledged loyalty
📝 Scoring Note
This is a sensitive topic. Students should understand that Spain's policy was strategic — it weakened British colonies while growing Spanish Florida's population and defenses.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose C may think Florida had no governing authority. Those who choose A are trivializing the motivations of people seeking freedom from enslavement.
5What was life like for pioneers in territorial Florida?
  • A) Easy and comfortable with modern conveniences
  • B) Challenging — families built homesteads, farmed, and lived far from towns
  • C) Exactly like life in big cities
  • D) Pioneers did not come to Florida
✓ Correct Answer
B — Challenging — families built homesteads, farmed, and lived far from towns
📝 Scoring Note
Assesses understanding of daily life in territorial Florida and the realities of frontier settlement.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A or C may be projecting modern life onto the past — a common presentism error worth discussing.
6What does the word "territory" mean in U.S. history?
  • A) A state with full voting rights
  • B) Land controlled by the U.S. government that has not yet become a state
  • C) A country that belongs to another country
  • D) A city with its own laws
✓ Correct Answer
B — Land controlled by the U.S. government that has not yet become a state
📝 Scoring Note
Tests vocabulary comprehension of a key unit term. Students should distinguish territory from state.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may confuse territory and state. Those who choose C may be thinking of a colony rather than a territory.
7What can historians learn from studying the archaeology of Fort Mose?
  • A) Nothing — the site was destroyed
  • B) Evidence of how free Black settlers built homes, farmed, and defended their community
  • C) Only information about Spanish soldiers
  • D) How to build modern houses
✓ Correct Answer
B — Evidence of how free Black settlers built homes, farmed, and defended their community
📝 Scoring Note
Assesses understanding of archaeology as a tool for learning about the past, especially for communities whose written records are limited.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may not understand that archaeological sites can yield evidence even when structures are gone. Those who choose C may not realize the settlement was primarily a free Black community.
8Why was Florida's location important to the United States in the early 1800s?
  • A) The U.S. wanted Florida's gold mines
  • B) Florida's coastline and borders were important for security, trade, and expansion
  • C) Florida had the largest population
  • D) The U.S. needed more desert land
✓ Correct Answer
B — Florida's coastline and borders were important for security, trade, and expansion
📝 Scoring Note
Connects geography to political decision-making — students should understand strategic reasons for U.S. interest in Florida.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may be confusing Florida with western territories. Those who choose D may not understand Florida's actual geography (subtropical, not desert).

Section B
Source Task — Fort Mose
📄
Historical Account — Fort Mose
"In 1738, the governor of Spanish Florida established Gracia Real de Santa Teresa de Mose — known as Fort Mose — about two miles north of St. Augustine. It became a community of formerly enslaved people who had escaped from British colonies. The residents built homes, grew crops, and formed a militia to help defend St. Augustine. Fort Mose is recognized as the first legally sanctioned free Black settlement in what is now the United States."
1Based on the source, what did the people of Fort Mose do to build their community? Name at least two things.
✓ Model Answer
"The people of Fort Mose built homes, grew crops, and formed a militia to help defend St. Augustine. They created a real community where they could live as free people."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for at least two specific actions from the source (built homes, grew crops, formed a militia). Full credit = two or more actions cited from the source. Partial credit = only one action or vague reference without specifics.
2Why is Fort Mose an important historical site? Use evidence from the source to explain.
✓ Model Answer
"Fort Mose is important because it was the first legally sanctioned free Black settlement in what is now the United States. The source shows that formerly enslaved people did not just escape — they built a functioning community with homes, farms, and a militia."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for the key claim (first free Black settlement) supported by evidence from the source. Full credit = significance stated + source evidence. Partial = significance stated without evidence, or evidence without clear statement of significance.
C
Section C
Socratic Response — Claim & Evidence
✍Florida changed hands multiple times — from Spain to Britain to Spain to the United States. Think about how these changes affected the people living in Florida, including Native peoples, free Black communities like Fort Mose, and settlers. Choose one group and explain how a change in control might have affected their lives. Use evidence from what you learned.
✓ Model Answer
"When Florida changed from Spanish to British control, the people of Fort Mose were deeply affected. Under Spain, they had been free. But Britain supported slavery, so the free Black community at Fort Mose was in danger. Many residents had to flee to Cuba when Britain took over. This shows how a change in colonial power could mean the difference between freedom and enslavement for real people."
📝 Scoring Note
Accept any of the three groups (Native peoples, free Black communities, settlers). The key is specific evidence and a clear explanation of cause and effect. Full credit = group chosen + evidence + explanation of impact. Partial = group chosen but explanation is vague, or evidence is missing.

Section D
Virtue Lens — Character Reflection
🏭 Virtue Prompt — Courage
Think about the virtue of courage. How did the people who escaped slavery and built Fort Mose show courage? What risks did they take, and why was their decision brave?
✓ Model Answer
"The people who escaped to Fort Mose showed incredible courage. They risked their lives to escape slavery in British colonies, traveling through dangerous territory to reach Spanish Florida. Even after arriving, they faced the threat of attack. But they chose freedom and built a community where they could live with dignity. Their courage is especially remarkable because they knew the risks — capture could mean punishment or death — yet they chose to fight for their freedom anyway."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for: (1) The virtue of courage identified, (2) Specific risks or actions connected to the people of Fort Mose, (3) An explanation of why their actions were brave. Full credit = all three. Partial = courage mentioned but connection is vague or lacks specific risks.
