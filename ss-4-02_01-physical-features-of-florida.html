Geographers don't quit when the land gets tricky.

🔎 Discovery Fact
The word "terrain" means the physical features of a piece of land. Florida's terrain is one of the flattest in the country — its highest point is only 345 feet above sea level!

The Shape of Florida
Peninsula, panhandle, coastlines, and wetlands

Florida is shaped like a long finger pointing south into warm water. The Gulf of Mexico sits to the west, the Atlantic Ocean to the east, and the Straits of Florida curve along the south. Because three sides of Florida touch water, geographers call it a peninsula — a piece of land with water almost all the way around it.

The narrow western part of the state is called the panhandle — it sticks out like the handle of a pan. Across Florida you'll find long sandy beaches, slow rivers, hundreds of clear springs, the giant inland Lake Okeechobee, and the vast wetlands of the Everglades in the south. Most of the land is low and flat, and the climate is warm and rainy — which is why Florida has so many wetlands and so much green even in winter.

🤔

Pause & Think: Look at a map in your head. If Florida were not a peninsula, how would life here be different? Would people travel, fish, or build homes in the same places?

💡 Key Concept

Florida is a low, flat peninsula with two long coastlines, a panhandle, hundreds of springs and lakes, and the great wetlands of the Everglades. The land's shape and water shape how people have lived here from the start.

Quick Self-Check
🧠

Not graded — just for you! Answer these three questions to see how much you remember before writing your evidence responses.

1. Which word best describes Florida's shape?
AA peninsula
BAn island
CA mountain range
Correct! Florida is a peninsula — a piece of land with water on three sides. The Gulf of Mexico, the Atlantic Ocean, and the Straits of Florida surround it.
Look back at the physical map. An island has water all the way around it. A peninsula has water on three sides but is still connected to the mainland. Which one fits Florida?
2. What is the Everglades?
AA mountain in the panhandle
BA large wetland in southern Florida
CA city near the coast
Correct! The Everglades is a giant wetland in southern Florida — slow-moving water, sawgrass, and a habitat for alligators, panthers, and wading birds.
Remember: Florida is low and flat — no real mountains. Think about what fills the southern part of the state. It's wet, green, and full of wildlife.
3. Why does the panhandle have its name?
ABecause it has many farms
BBecause it sticks out like the handle of a pan
CBecause it is the oldest part of Florida
Correct! Look at the map — the western part of Florida is long and narrow, like the handle of a pan attached to the round body of the state.
Imagine looking at a pan from above — a round body with a long handle sticking out. Which part of Florida looks like that handle?
