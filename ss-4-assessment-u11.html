Source & Map Task

Historical Source: Florida Lunch Counter Sit-Ins (1960)

Read the following account describing the 1960 lunch counter sit-ins in Florida.

“In March 1960, a group of college students in Tallahassee walked into a Woolworth’s store and sat down at the whites-only lunch counter. They were refused service, but they stayed seated — calm, quiet, and peaceful. Some people shouted at them. Others poured food on them. But the students did not fight back. They returned the next day, and the day after that. Their peaceful protest helped show the nation that segregation was unfair. Within a few years, laws began to change.”
9Use the source above to answer the following question.
What strategy did the students use to protest segregation? Use details from the source to explain.
10Think about what you read in the source.
Why do you think the students chose to remain peaceful even when people treated them badly?
11Connect the source to what you learned in Unit 11.
Based on this source and what you learned in Unit 11, how did ordinary citizens help bring about change in Florida?
