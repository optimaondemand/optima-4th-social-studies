📝 Assessment Instructions

This assessment covers Unit 10 — Modern Florida Growth. Show what you know about the railroads, industries, immigrants, and key individuals that helped Florida grow from the late 1800s through the Space Age.

  • Read each question carefully before choosing or writing your answer
  • Choose the best answer for each multiple-choice question
  • Write in complete sentences for all open-response questions
  • Review your answers before submitting

Knowledge Check

1Henry Flagler is BEST known for:
  • A) Discovering gold in central Florida
  • B) Building railroads and hotels along Florida’s east coast
  • C) Serving as Florida’s first governor
  • D) Leading the Seminole Wars
2What role did Tampa play during the Spanish-American War of 1898?
  • A) It was the site of a major sea battle
  • B) It served as the main launching point for U.S. troops going to Cuba
  • C) It was captured by Spanish forces
  • D) It became the new state capital
3Immigrants from Cuba, Spain, Italy, and other countries contributed to Florida’s growth by:
  • A) Building all of Florida’s schools
  • B) Working in industries like cigar-making in Ybor City and bringing new cultural traditions
  • C) Serving as Florida’s governors
  • D) Replacing all Native American communities
4The Florida land boom of the 1920s ended in a “bust” mainly because:
  • A) People stopped wanting to live in warm places
  • B) Land prices were driven too high by speculation, and hurricanes damaged the area
  • C) The government banned all building in Florida
  • D) Railroads stopped running to Florida
5Mary McLeod Bethune is remembered for:
  • A) Building Florida’s first railroad
  • B) Fighting for education and civil rights, founding a school for Black students
  • C) Discovering the Everglades
  • D) Inventing the telegraph
6How did the New Deal help Florida during the Great Depression?
  • A) It closed all Florida businesses
  • B) It created jobs through public works projects like building roads, parks, and bridges
  • C) It moved all Floridians to other states
  • D) It ended tourism permanently
7Julia Tuttle is known as the “Mother of Miami” because she:
  • A) Was the first person born in Miami
  • B) Convinced Henry Flagler to extend his railroad to Miami
  • C) Built Miami’s first school
  • D) Served as Miami’s first mayor
8Florida’s space program at Cape Canaveral became important because:
  • A) It was the only flat area in the United States
  • B) It helped the U.S. compete in the Space Race and brought jobs and technology to Florida
  • C) It replaced all other Florida industries
  • D) It was built during the Civil War

Section B
Source & Map Task
⏲ ~12 min
📜
Historical Source: Ybor City Cigar Workers (c. 1910)

Read the following description of an early 1900s photograph showing Ybor City cigar workers.

“This photograph from around 1910 shows the inside of a cigar factory in Ybor City, Tampa. Rows of workers — men and women from Cuba, Spain, and Italy — sit at long wooden tables, carefully rolling cigars by hand. In the corner, a man called the ‘lector’ reads aloud from a newspaper to the workers while they work. The factories employed thousands of immigrants and became the center of a thriving multicultural neighborhood with its own restaurants, social clubs, and traditions.”
9Use the source above to answer the following question.
What does this source tell us about the daily lives of immigrant workers in Ybor City?
10Think about the role of the lector described in the source.
How does the role of the “lector” show that these workers valued education and staying informed?
11Connect the source to what you learned in Unit 10.
Based on this source and what you learned in Unit 10, how did immigrant communities help Florida grow?

12Read the prompt below and write a multi-sentence response.
Henry Flagler, Henry Plant, Julia Tuttle, and Mary McLeod Bethune all helped Florida grow in different ways. Choose TWO of these individuals. Explain what each person did and how their contributions were similar or different.
13Read the prompt below and write a multi-sentence response.
Florida went through a boom in the 1920s, a bust, the Great Depression, and then growth again after WWII. What does this pattern teach us about how economies change over time?

🏆 Virtue Focus: Perseverance
Perseverance means keeping going even when things are very hard — not giving up when the path is difficult.
14Reflect on what you learned in Unit 10.
Mary McLeod Bethune started a school with almost no money, using crates for desks and charcoal for pencils. Think about what perseverance means — keeping going even when things are very hard. Describe a time from Unit 10 when someone or a community showed perseverance, and explain why this virtue matters.
