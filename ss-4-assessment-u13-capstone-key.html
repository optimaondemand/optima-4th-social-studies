2
Part Two
Exhibition Exhibit Card — Exemplar Responses
9 points
Q1: What happened? Describe your chosen event or topic. Include specific facts — who, what, when, and where.
✅ Exemplar Response
Fort Mose was established in 1738 near St. Augustine, Florida. It was the first legally sanctioned free Black settlement in what is now the United States. Enslaved people from the British colonies escaped south to Spanish Florida, where they were granted freedom if they converted to Catholicism and served in the militia. Fort Mose became a community where free Black families lived, worked, and defended the Spanish border.
Q2: Why did it matter? Explain the significance using cause and effect.
✅ Exemplar Response
Fort Mose mattered because it showed that people fought for freedom long before the American Revolution. It was a cause-and-effect chain: British slavery caused people to flee, Spanish policy offered freedom, and that led to a new settlement. Fort Mose proved that the desire for liberty shaped Florida's history from its earliest colonial days.
Q3: What source or evidence supports your exhibit? Name a specific source and explain what it showed you.
✅ Exemplar Response
We studied a historical map showing Fort Mose's location north of St. Augustine. The map showed how the fort was positioned as a defensive outpost between British and Spanish territory. This helped me understand that Fort Mose wasn't just a community — it was also a military strategy.
📝 Scoring Note
Secure (3): Names specific facts (date, location, people), explains clear cause-to-effect, cites a specific source. Developing (2): Has the right idea but vague on details or cause-effect. Beginning (1): Very brief, missing key facts or no cause-effect. Score each prompt separately (3 pts each, 9 pts total).
3
Part Three
Historical Connections — Exemplar Responses
6 points
Q4: How did geography shape Florida's history? Give at least two specific examples from different time periods.
✅ Exemplar Response
Florida's long coastline and warm climate attracted explorers like Ponce de León, who was looking for new lands and resources. The coast made Florida easy to reach by ship, which is why so many European countries wanted to claim it. Later, Florida's flat land and sunny weather caused the Land Boom of the 1920s, when thousands of people moved to Florida to buy land and build homes. Geography shaped both who came to Florida and when they came.
Q5: Choose one person from Florida history. What virtue did they show, and how did their actions change Florida?
✅ Exemplar Response
Mary McLeod Bethune showed the virtue of perseverance. She grew up in a time when Black children had very few chances to go to school, but she believed education could change lives. She started a school for Black girls in Daytona Beach with just $1.50 and five students. Her school eventually became Bethune-Cookman University. Her perseverance changed Florida because she proved that one person's determination can create opportunities for thousands of others.
📝 Scoring Note
Q4: Look for two specific examples from different units/time periods. Q5: Look for a named virtue with reasoning that connects to the person's actions. 3 points per prompt.
4
Part Four
Historian-Citizen Reflection — Exemplar Responses
6 points
Q6: What does it mean to be a Florida historian-citizen? What skill are you most proud of developing this year?
✅ Exemplar Response
Being a Florida historian-citizen means understanding where Florida came from so you can help decide where it goes next. The skill I'm most proud of is learning to read primary sources. At the beginning of the year, old documents seemed confusing, but now I can look at a historical map or letter and figure out what it's telling me. That skill helps me think carefully about evidence, not just believe everything I hear.
Q7: If you could add one more file to the Florida Files, what would it be and why?
✅ Exemplar Response
I would add a file about Florida's space program at Cape Canaveral. Florida's location near the equator made it the perfect place to launch rockets, and the space program brought thousands of jobs and new technology to the state. It connects geography, economics, and innovation — three big themes from our year.
📝 Scoring Note
Look for a meaningful definition of historian-citizen, a specific skill with growth evidence, and a creative file idea with reasoning that connects to course themes. 3 points per prompt.
