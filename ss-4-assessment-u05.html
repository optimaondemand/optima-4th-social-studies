A
Section A
Knowledge Check
⏱️ ~8 Min
💡

Choose the best answer for each question. Click on the answer you think is correct — it will highlight green when selected.

1 Which geographic feature made Florida important to early European explorers?
  • A) Its large mountain ranges
  • B) Its location between the Atlantic Ocean and Gulf of Mexico
  • C) Its cold winter climate
  • D) Its deep river canyons
2 How did Florida's environment shape the way the Calusa people lived?
  • A) They became mountain farmers
  • B) They relied on fishing and shellfish from coastal waters
  • C) They moved to colder regions each winter
  • D) They built stone castles for protection
3 What was one reason European explorers came to the Americas?
  • A) To learn the Timucua language
  • B) To find gold, new trade routes, and claim land
  • C) To escape Florida's hurricanes
  • D) To study alligators
4 What happened to many Native peoples after European contact?
  • A) They gained new lands and power
  • B) Nothing changed for them
  • C) Many suffered from diseases, lost land, and faced forced labor
  • D) They all moved to Europe
5 Why is it important to study history from more than one point of view?
  • A) Because only explorers wrote things down
  • B) Because different sources help us understand the full story
  • C) Because maps are always wrong
  • D) Because Native peoples did not have a history
6 Which of the following is an example of a PRIMARY source?
  • A) A textbook written in 2024
  • B) A diary entry from a Spanish explorer in 1565
  • C) A movie about the Timucua
  • D) A student's homework assignment
B
Section B
Source & Map Task
⏱️ ~10 Min
🔍

Source Study: Comparing Perspectives on Contact
Read the two short accounts below about European arrival in Florida. Then answer the questions using details from each source.

📜
Source A — Explorer's Log (1513)

Type: Primary Source  |  Author: Spanish Explorer  |  Date: 1513

"We have found a beautiful land with tall trees and clear water. We claimed it for Spain and named it La Florida."
🪶
Source B — Native Elder's Account (retold)

Type: Oral History (retold)  |  Perspective: Indigenous Community

"Strangers arrived in great boats. They planted a flag and said this land was theirs — but it had been ours for thousands of years."
✍️

Now use what you read to answer the two questions below. Try to include specific details from each source in your responses.

Evidence Question 1: What does Source A tell us about how the explorer saw Florida? Use a detail from the source.
Evidence Question 2: How is Source B's perspective different from Source A? What does this difference teach us about studying history?
C
Section C
Socratic Response
⏱️ ~12 Min
🧠

Think about everything you have learned about Florida's land, its first peoples, and the arrival of European explorers. This is your chance to make a claim and defend it with evidence!

⭐

Strong responses include: A clear claim (your position), at least two pieces of evidence from what you learned, and an explanation of what your evidence shows.

Socratic Response: Did European contact change Florida for the better, for the worse, or was it complicated? Make a claim and support it with at least two pieces of evidence from what you have learned.

Section D
Virtue Lens Reflection
⏱️ ~5 Min
⚖️ Virtue Lens
Historians don't just record events — they also ask: Was this right? Was this just? Did people show courage or honesty? The Virtue Lens helps us think about the character behind historical actions. Choose one virtue below and connect it to something you studied in this unit.
🦁
Courage
Facing fear or risk to do what is right
⚖️
Justice
Treating people fairly and standing up for what is right
🕊️
Honesty
Telling the truth and being transparent, even when it's hard
💭

Did a person or group show this virtue? Or was it missing in a situation you studied? Either answer can lead to a great reflection!

Virtue Lens Reflection: Choose one virtue — courage, justice, or honesty — and explain how it connects to something you learned in this unit.
