Word Roots
Greek Root: metron — "measure"

The Greek root metron means "measure." Latitude is a measurement. Longitude is a measurement. Scale is a measurement. Every Map Quest tool is rooted in this idea.

📖

🔎 Discovery Fact
The word geometry means "earth-measure" — geo (earth) + metron (measure). The math of measuring shapes started as a way of measuring land, which is why geographers and geometers share a root.

Learn the Story

The Four Tools of a Map Quest
Latitude, longitude, scale, and symbols

Latitude lines run east-west — they tell you how far north or south a place is. The equator is 0°. Florida sits between about 24°N and 31°N. Longitude lines run north-south — they tell you how far east or west a place is. Florida sits between about 80°W and 87°W. When you give both numbers together — like 28°N, 81°W (close to Orlando) — you've named a single spot on Earth.

Scale tells you how distance on the map matches distance in real life. A scale bar might show that one inch on the map equals 50 miles on the ground. Symbols are the little marks in the legend — a star for the capital, a dot for a city, a wavy blue line for a river, a dashed line for a border. Together, these four tools — lat, long, scale, symbols — let you find, measure, and read any place on any map.

🤔

Pause & Think: Picture a Florida map in your head. If someone says "go to 25°N, 80°W," can you guess what part of Florida they're pointing to? (Hint: that's near Miami.) That's the power of latitude and longitude.

💡 Key Concept

Every Florida map can be read with four tools: latitude (north-south position), longitude (east-west position), scale (real distance), and symbols (the legend). A geographer who uses all four can find, measure, and interpret any place on any Florida map.

Quick Self-Check

Not graded — just for you! Answer these three questions to see how much you remember before writing your evidence responses.

1. What do latitude lines tell you?
AHow far east or west a place is
BHow far north or south a place is
CThe weather at a place
Correct! Latitude lines run east-west across the map, and they measure how far north or south a place is from the equator.
Picture lines that run across the map horizontally — like the rungs of a ladder. Are they showing east-west position, or are they showing how far up or down you are?
2. What does a scale bar on a map tell you?
AThe temperature of the region
BHow map distance matches real distance
CThe names of the cities
Correct! A scale bar shows how distance on the map equals real-world distance. One inch might equal 50 miles on the ground.
Think about what the word scale means. It's about comparing two things — what's on the map and what's in real life. Which option fits that comparison?
3. What is the legend on a map?
AA short story about the map's history
BA list of all the cities shown
CThe key that explains what each symbol means
Correct! The legend (sometimes called the key) lists every symbol on the map and tells you what each one means — a star is the capital, a dot is a city, and so on.
The legend is the part of the map that helps you read the OTHER parts. Which option is the explainer for the rest of the map's marks?
