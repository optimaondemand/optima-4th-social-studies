Section A
Multiple Choice — Knowledge & Recall
1Which geographic feature made Florida important to early European explorers?
  • A) Its large mountain ranges
  • B) Its location between the Atlantic Ocean and Gulf of Mexico
  • C) Its cold winter climate
  • D) Its deep river canyons
✓ Correct Answer
B — Its location between the Atlantic Ocean and Gulf of Mexico
📝 Scoring Note
Students should recognize Florida's peninsular location as strategically important for sea routes.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A or D may be confusing Florida with other U.S. regions.
2How did Florida's environment shape the way the Calusa people lived?
  • A) They became mountain farmers
  • B) They relied on fishing and shellfish from coastal waters
  • C) They moved to colder regions each winter
  • D) They built stone castles for protection
✓ Correct Answer
B — They relied on fishing and shellfish from coastal waters
📝 Scoring Note
Connects geography to human adaptation — a key synthesis skill.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may not yet distinguish Florida's flat, coastal geography from mountainous regions.
3What was one reason European explorers came to the Americas?
  • A) To learn the Timucua language
  • B) To find gold, new trade routes, and claim land
  • C) To escape Florida's hurricanes
  • D) To study alligators
✓ Correct Answer
B — To find gold, new trade routes, and claim land
📝 Scoring Note
Assesses understanding of European motives (gold, God, glory, trade).
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who pick humorous distractors (C or D) may need review of Unit 4 content.
4What happened to many Native peoples after European contact?
  • A) They gained new lands and power
  • B) Nothing changed for them
  • C) Many suffered from diseases, lost land, and faced forced labor
  • D) They all moved to Europe
✓ Correct Answer
C — Many suffered from diseases, lost land, and faced forced labor
📝 Scoring Note
This is a sensitive topic. Look for accurate understanding without oversimplification.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A or B may hold misconceptions about the impact of colonization.
5Why is it important to study history from more than one point of view?
  • A) Because only explorers wrote things down
  • B) Because different sources help us understand the full story
  • C) Because maps are always wrong
  • D) Because Native peoples did not have a history
✓ Correct Answer
B — Because different sources help us understand the full story
📝 Scoring Note
Tests understanding of multiperspectivity — a core historical thinking skill.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose D hold a harmful misconception that should be addressed directly.
6Which of the following is an example of a PRIMARY source?
  • A) A textbook written in 2024
  • B) A diary entry from a Spanish explorer in 1565
  • C) A movie about the Timucua
  • D) A student's homework assignment
✓ Correct Answer
B — A diary entry from a Spanish explorer in 1565
📝 Scoring Note
Reviews Unit 1 skill of distinguishing primary vs. secondary sources.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A or C may confuse primary and secondary sources.
B
Section B
Source Task — Evidence & Perspective
📄
Source A — Explorer's Log (1513)
"We have found a beautiful land with tall trees and clear water. We claimed it for Spain and named it La Florida."
👤
Source B — Native Elder's Account (retold)
"Strangers arrived in great boats. They planted a flag and said this land was theirs — but it had been ours for thousands of years."
1What does Source A tell us about how the explorer saw Florida? Use a detail from the source.
✓ Model Answer
"Source A shows the explorer saw Florida as a beautiful, unclaimed land. The detail 'tall trees and clear water' shows he admired the environment, and 'claimed it for Spain' shows he believed he had the right to take it. The explorer's perspective does not mention the people already living there."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for at least one specific detail from Source A and an inference about the explorer's perspective. Full credit = detail + inference. Partial credit = detail only or inference only.
2How is Source B's perspective different from Source A? What does this difference teach us about studying history?
✓ Model Answer
"Source B is different because the Native elder sees the Europeans as 'strangers' who took land that was not theirs. While Source A treats the land as new and unclaimed, Source B reminds us it had been home to Native peoples 'for thousands of years.' This difference teaches us that one event can look very different depending on who is telling the story, which is why historians study multiple perspectives."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for a clear contrast between the two perspectives and a connection to why multiple viewpoints matter. Full credit = contrast + lesson about perspective. Partial = one or the other.
C
Section C
Socratic Response — Claim & Evidence
✍Think about everything you have learned about Florida's land, its first peoples, and the arrival of European explorers. Make a claim: Did European contact change Florida for the better, for the worse, or was it complicated? Support your claim with at least two pieces of evidence from what you have learned.
✓ Model Answer
"European contact changed Florida in complicated ways. On one hand, explorers brought new tools, animals, and connections to a larger world. On the other hand, many Native peoples like the Calusa and Timucua suffered greatly — they lost their lands, were forced into labor, and many died from European diseases they had never encountered before. The land itself was also changed as Europeans built settlements and missions. Because there were both gains and losses depending on whose perspective you consider, the answer is not simple. This is why it is important to study history from more than one point of view."
📝 Scoring Note
Accept any well-supported claim (better, worse, or complicated). The key is evidence from multiple units. Look for: (1) A clear claim, (2) At least 2 pieces of evidence, (3) Evidence drawn from more than one unit's content. Full credit = all three. Partial = claim + 1 evidence or 2 evidence without clear claim.
D
Section D
Virtue Lens — Character Reflection
🏭 Virtue Prompt
Choose one virtue — courage, justice, or honesty — and explain how it connects to something you learned in this unit. For example, did a person or group show this virtue? Was this virtue missing in a situation you studied?
✓ Model Answer
"Justice connects to what we learned about European contact with Native peoples. When explorers claimed land that already belonged to the Calusa and Timucua, that was an example of justice being missing. The Native peoples had lived on and cared for their land for thousands of years, but explorers took it without asking. Studying this helps us think about what fairness means and why it matters to hear everyone's side of a story."
📝 Scoring Note
Accept any of the three virtues. Look for: (1) A named virtue, (2) A specific connection to unit content, (3) Some explanation of how the virtue applies. Full credit = all three. Partial = virtue named but connection is vague.
