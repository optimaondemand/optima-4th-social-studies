Learn the Story
📖
Three Branches, One Government
How Florida divides the work of governing

Florida's state government is divided into three branches. Each branch has a different job, and no branch can do everything on its own. This separation of powers keeps any one person or group from having too much control. It's one of the most important ideas in American government.

The Legislative Branch makes the laws. In Florida, the legislature has two chambers: the Senate (with 40 senators) and the House of Representatives (with 120 representatives). These legislators are elected by the people. They write bills, debate them, and vote on whether they become law. Topics range from education and the environment to roads and public safety.

The Executive Branch carries out the laws. The governor is the head of the executive branch. The governor signs bills into law, manages state agencies (like the Department of Education and the Department of Environmental Protection), and represents Florida in national discussions. The governor also has the power to veto (reject) a bill passed by the legislature.

The Judicial Branch interprets the laws. Florida's courts — from local circuit courts to the Florida Supreme Court — decide whether laws are being applied fairly and whether they follow the constitution. Judges do not make laws, but they can rule that a law is unconstitutional — meaning it breaks the rules set by the Florida Constitution.

Checks and balances. The three branches watch each other. If the legislature passes a bad law, the governor can veto it. If the governor oversteps, the legislature can investigate. If either branch breaks the constitution, the courts can step in. This system protects citizens by making sure power is shared, not concentrated.

💡 Key Concept

Florida's three branches of government — legislative, executive, and judicial — have different roles that help organize power and responsibility.

🌴 Opti says…
Think of it like a three-legged stool: if you remove one leg, the whole thing falls over. Each branch holds up its part of the government so no one person carries too much weight.
