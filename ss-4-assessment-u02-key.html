Knowledge Check
1What makes Florida a peninsula?
  • A) It is surrounded by mountains on three sides
  • B) It is a flat piece of land with no rivers
  • C) It is land surrounded by water on three sides ✓
  • D) It is the largest state in the Southeast
Correct Answer
C) It is land surrounded by water on three sides.
Common Misconception
Students sometimes think peninsula means 'island' or confuse it with an isthmus. Remind them: a peninsula is connected to land on one side.
2What does a political map show?
  • A) Mountains, rivers, and lakes
  • B) Boundaries, cities, and capitals ✓
  • C) Temperature and rainfall
  • D) Highways and railroads only
Correct Answer
B) Boundaries, cities, and capitals.
Common Misconception
Students often confuse political and physical maps. Political = human-made boundaries; physical = natural landforms.
3What does a physical map show?
  • A) City names and state capitals
  • B) Roads and highways
  • C) Landforms, rivers, and elevation ✓
  • D) Population numbers
Correct Answer
C) Landforms, rivers, and elevation.
Common Misconception
Same confusion as Q2. Reinforce: physical maps show what the land looks like naturally.
4What is the difference between weather and climate?
  • A) Weather and climate mean the same thing
  • B) Weather is what you see today; climate is the long-term pattern of weather in a place ✓
  • C) Climate changes every day; weather stays the same
  • D) Weather is only about temperature; climate is only about rain
Correct Answer
B) Weather is what you see today; climate is the long-term pattern of weather in a place.
Common Misconception
Students frequently think weather and climate are the same. Use the analogy: weather is today's outfit; climate is your whole wardrobe.
5What is the Florida Panhandle?
  • A) A tool used by Florida mapmakers
  • B) The narrow strip of land in northwest Florida ✓
  • C) A river in central Florida
  • D) The southern tip of the state
Correct Answer
B) The narrow strip of land in northwest Florida.
Common Misconception
Students may think the Panhandle is in south Florida or confuse it with the Florida Keys.
6What does latitude measure?
  • A) How far east or west a place is
  • B) How high above sea level a place is
  • C) How far north or south of the equator a place is ✓
  • D) How many miles between two cities
Correct Answer
C) How far north or south of the equator a place is.
Common Misconception
Students commonly confuse latitude and longitude. Memory aid: latitude lines are like a ladder (horizontal rungs).
7What is a wetland?
  • A) A desert area with very little water
  • B) An area where land is covered by shallow water, at least part of the year ✓
  • C) A deep ocean trench
  • D) A mountain lake
Correct Answer
B) An area where land is covered by shallow water, at least part of the year.
Common Misconception
Students may think wetlands are always underwater or confuse them with only swamps. Wetlands include marshes, bogs, and swamps.
8What does a map legend do?
  • A) Shows the title of the map
  • B) Tells you when the map was made
  • C) Explains the symbols used on the map ✓
  • D) Shows the latitude and longitude lines
Correct Answer
C) Explains the symbols used on the map.
Common Misconception
Students sometimes confuse the legend with the title or the scale. The legend (or key) decodes the symbols.

Section B
Source & Map Task
📄
Source Scenario

Your teacher gives you two maps of Florida. Map A shows colored regions for elevation — green lowlands along the coast and brown highlands in the north. Map B shows city names, county lines, and a star marking the state capital.

Use what you know about maps and Florida's geography to answer the questions below.
9Which map is the physical map and which is the political map? How do you know?
Model Answer (Secure — 3)
Map A is the physical map because it shows elevation with colors — green for lowlands and brown for highlands. These are natural features of the land. Map B is the political map because it shows city names, county lines, and a star for the state capital. These are human-made boundaries and labels, not natural features.
Scoring Notes
Secure (3): Correctly identifies Map A as physical and Map B as political; gives clear evidence for each (natural features vs. human-made boundaries). Developing (2): Identifies both correctly but only explains one, or explanation is vague. Beginning (1): Mislabels one or both maps, or provides no explanation.
10Using what you know about Florida's physical features, explain why most of Florida's big cities are near the coast.
Model Answer (Secure — 3)
Most of Florida's big cities are near the coast because the coastline provides many advantages. Florida is a peninsula, so there is a long coastline with access to the ocean. People settled near the coast for fishing, trade, and shipping. The flat, low-lying coastal areas also made it easier to build. Ports like Tampa, Miami, and Jacksonville grew because ships could bring goods in and out. The coast also attracts tourism because of the beaches, which brings jobs and money.
Scoring Notes
Secure (3): Names at least 2 reasons connecting physical features (coastline, flat land, ports, ocean access) to city locations. Developing (2): Names 1 reason or gives a general answer without referencing specific physical features. Beginning (1): Cannot connect geography to city placement.
11If you wanted to find out how far it is from Tallahassee to Miami, which map tool would you use? Explain.
Model Answer (Secure — 3)
I would use the map scale. A map scale shows the relationship between distance on the map and real distance on the ground. For example, the scale might show that one inch on the map equals 100 miles in real life. I would measure the distance between Tallahassee and Miami on the map, then use the scale to convert that measurement into real miles.
Scoring Notes
Secure (3): Names the scale; explains how it converts map distance to real distance with an example. Developing (2): Names the scale but cannot fully explain how to use it. Beginning (1): Names the wrong tool (legend, compass rose) or gives no explanation.
C
Section C
Socratic Response
12Florida has a warm, humid climate with a hurricane season from June through November. How does Florida's climate affect the way people live, build homes, and plan their activities? Give at least two examples.
Model Answer (Secure — 3)
Florida's warm, humid climate affects daily life in many ways. First, homes in Florida are often built with strong materials and hurricane shutters because hurricane season brings powerful storms with high winds and flooding. Builders also raise some homes off the ground to protect against storm surge. Second, people plan outdoor activities around the climate — they might schedule events in the cooler winter months instead of the hot, rainy summer. Schools and businesses also have hurricane preparedness plans. Air conditioning is essential in Florida homes because of the heat and humidity.
Scoring Notes
Secure (3): Gives 2+ specific examples connecting climate to daily life (building codes, hurricane prep, scheduling, AC, raised homes, etc.). Developing (2): Gives only 1 specific example or examples are vague. Beginning (1): Cannot connect climate to how people live.
13Explain the difference between a cultural feature and a physical feature. Give two examples of each that you would find in Florida.
Model Answer (Secure — 3)
A physical feature is something created by nature — it is part of the natural landscape. Examples in Florida include the Everglades wetlands and Lake Okeechobee. A cultural feature is something created by people — it shows how humans have changed or used the land. Examples in Florida include the city of Tallahassee (the state capital) and Interstate 95 (a major highway). The difference is that physical features exist without human help, while cultural features are built or created by people.
Scoring Notes
Secure (3): Clearly defines both types; gives 2 accurate Florida examples of each; explains the difference. Developing (2): Defines both but gives only 1 example per type, or one example is inaccurate. Beginning (1): Cannot distinguish between the two types or gives no Florida examples.

Section D
Virtue Lens Reflection
⭐ Unit Virtue — Perseverance
Perseverance means keeping going even when things are hard. Floridians show perseverance when they face challenges like hurricanes and keep rebuilding.
14The virtue for this unit is perseverance. Think about Florida's hurricane season. How do Floridians show perseverance when facing storms and rebuilding after damage?
Model Answer (Secure — 3)
Floridians show perseverance during hurricane season by preparing before storms, staying strong during them, and rebuilding after the damage. Before a hurricane, families board up windows, stock up on supplies, and make evacuation plans. After a storm, communities come together to clean up, repair homes, and reopen schools and businesses — even when the damage is severe. Floridians do not give up; they keep rebuilding year after year because they love their state and their communities. This is perseverance because it means continuing to work hard and stay hopeful even when things are very difficult.
Scoring Notes
Secure (3): Connects perseverance to specific actions during hurricane season (preparation, rebuilding, community support) with reasoning. Developing (2): Mentions perseverance and hurricanes but actions are vague or reasoning is thin. Beginning (1): Does not connect perseverance to hurricane recovery.
