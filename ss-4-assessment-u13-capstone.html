🎉 Welcome to the Florida History Exhibition!

Congratulations, historian! You've spent the entire year exploring Florida's incredible story — from the First Peoples thousands of years ago to the Civil Rights Movement and beyond. Now it's time to show everything you've learned by creating your own Florida History Exhibition.

This capstone brings together all your knowledge, skills, and thinking from every unit. Take your time, write thoughtfully, and show what a true Florida historian-citizen can do!

  • Put 10 Florida history events in the correct timeline order
  • Create a museum-quality exhibit card about a topic you choose
  • Make connections across Florida's entire history
  • Reflect on your year as a Florida historian-citizen
  • Gather your answers, copy, and submit

Part Two
Florida Historian's Exhibition
⏲ ~15 min
💡

This is the heart of your exhibition! Choose ONE event, person, or topic from any unit this year. You'll create an exhibit card — just like a real museum historian.

1 What happened? Write 3–4 sentences with key facts about your chosen topic.
Describe your chosen event, person, or topic with specific facts.
2 Why did it matter? Explain the cause and effect — what led to this event, and what changed because of it?
Explain what caused it and what changed because of it.
3 What source or evidence helped you understand this topic? Describe a primary source, map, or document you studied and what it showed you.
Name a specific source and explain what it taught you.
