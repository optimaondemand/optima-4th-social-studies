Section A
Multiple Choice — Knowledge & Recall
1In what year did Florida become the 27th state?
  • A) 1819
  • B) 1838
  • C) 1845
  • D) 1861
✓ Correct Answer
C — 1845
📝 Scoring Note
Straightforward recall. Students should distinguish the statehood date from other key Florida dates.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A (1819) may be confusing statehood with the Adams-Onis Treaty when Spain ceded Florida to the U.S. Students who choose D (1861) may be confusing statehood with secession.
2What was the main reason Florida's economy depended on plantations before the Civil War?
  • A) Plantations grew oranges for export
  • B) Plantations used enslaved labor to grow cotton and other crops for profit
  • C) Plantations were run by the government
  • D) There were no other jobs in Florida
✓ Correct Answer
B — Plantations used enslaved labor to grow cotton and other crops for profit
📝 Scoring Note
Tests understanding that enslaved labor was the economic foundation of the plantation system — not simply the crops grown.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may associate Florida with oranges (a later industry) and not yet understand the antebellum cotton economy. Students who choose D may oversimplify the economic picture.
3What does the word "secede" mean?
  • A) To join a new country
  • B) To formally withdraw from a group or nation
  • C) To vote in an election
  • D) To sign a peace treaty
✓ Correct Answer
B — To formally withdraw from a group or nation
📝 Scoring Note
Vocabulary recall. Key term for understanding secession and the causes of the Civil War.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may confuse "secede" with "accede" or think secession means joining a different nation rather than leaving one.
4Why did Florida secede from the Union in 1861?
  • A) Florida wanted to end slavery
  • B) Florida wanted to protect the system of slavery and joined the Confederate States
  • C) Florida was forced to leave by the president
  • D) Florida wanted to become part of Spain again
✓ Correct Answer
B — Florida wanted to protect the system of slavery and joined the Confederate States
📝 Scoring Note
This is a central question. Students should understand that the defense of slavery was the primary cause of secession.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A have the cause reversed — secession was to preserve slavery, not end it. Students who choose C may not understand that secession was a choice made by state leaders, not forced by the federal government.
5How did Florida contribute to the Confederate war effort?
  • A) Florida sent no soldiers or supplies
  • B) Florida provided about 15,000 soldiers and supplied cattle and salt to Confederate armies
  • C) Florida only fought one battle
  • D) Florida supported the Union
✓ Correct Answer
B — Florida provided about 15,000 soldiers and supplied cattle and salt to Confederate armies
📝 Scoring Note
Tests specific knowledge of Florida's role. Students should know Florida contributed both manpower and critical supplies.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A or C may underestimate Florida's role because it was a smaller state. Students who choose D may confuse Florida's Confederate allegiance.
6What were the 13th, 14th, and 15th Amendments designed to do?
  • A) Create new states
  • B) End slavery, guarantee citizenship and equal protection, and protect voting rights
  • C) Increase taxes
  • D) Allow women to vote
✓ Correct Answer
B — End slavery, guarantee citizenship and equal protection, and protect voting rights
📝 Scoring Note
Students should be able to identify the purpose of the Reconstruction Amendments as a group.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose D may be confusing the 15th Amendment (race-based voting rights) with the 19th Amendment (women's suffrage). This is a common and understandable mix-up.
7What happened during Reconstruction (1865–1877)?
  • A) Nothing changed after the war
  • B) Formerly enslaved people gained new rights, new leaders emerged, but many gains were later rolled back
  • C) The South became wealthier than the North
  • D) All Confederate soldiers went to prison
✓ Correct Answer
B — Formerly enslaved people gained new rights, new leaders emerged, but many gains were later rolled back
📝 Scoring Note
This is a nuanced question. The correct answer captures both the progress and the rollback — students should understand Reconstruction as a period of gains that were not sustained.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may believe slavery's end immediately solved all problems. Students who choose D may have an oversimplified view of post-war consequences.
8Why is it important to study both the causes and effects of the Civil War?
  • A) Because the war was not important
  • B) Because understanding causes and effects helps us see how slavery, conflict, and justice are connected to today
  • C) Because only soldiers were affected
  • D) Because the war only lasted one year
✓ Correct Answer
B — Because understanding causes and effects helps us see how slavery, conflict, and justice are connected to today
📝 Scoring Note
Tests historical thinking — the ability to connect past events to present understanding. Accept any response that shows awareness of cause-and-effect reasoning.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose C may not yet understand the war's broad impact on all of society — enslaved people, families, communities, the economy, and the nation's laws. Students who choose D may not recall that the Civil War lasted four years (1861–1865).

Section B
Source Task — Evidence & Analysis
📄
Historical Source — Florida in 1860
"By 1860, Florida had about 140,000 people. Nearly half — about 62,000 — were enslaved African Americans. They worked on plantations growing cotton, sugar, and tobacco. Enslaved people had no freedom, no pay, and no legal rights. Families could be separated and sold at any time. Despite these conditions, enslaved people built communities, maintained cultural traditions, and resisted their bondage in many ways."
1What does this source tell you about the lives of enslaved people in Florida? Name at least two details.
✓ Model Answer
"The source tells us that enslaved people in Florida had no freedom, no pay, and no legal rights. Their families could be separated and sold. Nearly half of Florida's population was enslaved, and they worked on plantations growing cotton, sugar, and tobacco."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for at least two specific details drawn from the source text. Full credit = 2+ details with clear reference to the source. Partial credit = only 1 detail, or details are vague/not from the source.
2The source says enslaved people "resisted their bondage in many ways." Why do you think it is important for historians to include this information?
✓ Model Answer
"It is important because it shows that enslaved people were not passive — they actively resisted and maintained their cultures and communities despite terrible conditions. Including this information gives a fuller, more honest picture of history and honors the humanity and agency of enslaved people."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for understanding that (1) enslaved people had agency and (2) including resistance gives a more complete picture. Full credit = both ideas present. Partial = one idea or vague reasoning.
C
Section C
Socratic Response — Analysis & Reasoning
✍Florida seceded from the Union to protect slavery. After the war, Reconstruction brought new rights — but those rights were later rolled back. In your own words, explain: What changed during Reconstruction, and what happened when Reconstruction ended? Why does this pattern matter?
✓ Model Answer
"During Reconstruction, formerly enslaved people gained important new rights. The 13th Amendment ended slavery, the 14th gave citizenship and equal protection, and the 15th protected voting rights. New Black leaders were elected to office and schools were built. But when Reconstruction ended in 1877, many of these gains were rolled back through new laws and violence that took away Black citizens' rights. This pattern matters because it shows that progress is not automatic — rights have to be protected and defended."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for: (1) Specific gains during Reconstruction (amendments, new leaders, schools), (2) Understanding that gains were rolled back, (3) A reason why the pattern matters. Full credit = all three elements. Partial = addresses some but not all.
✍Think about what it means to be a state versus a territory. When Florida became a state in 1845, some people celebrated — but statehood also meant that slavery was protected by state law. Explain how statehood could mean different things for different groups of people in Florida.
✓ Model Answer
"When Florida became a state in 1845, white landowners and settlers may have celebrated because statehood gave them more political power and representation in Congress. But for enslaved people, statehood meant that the system of slavery was now protected by Florida's state government. For free Black Floridians, statehood could mean new restrictions. This shows that the same event can have very different meanings depending on who you are."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for: (1) At least two different groups identified, (2) How each group experienced statehood differently, (3) Recognition that the same event affects people unequally. Full credit = all three. Partial = mentions different groups but doesn't fully explain the differences.

Section D
Virtue Lens — Justice Reflection
⚖ Virtue Prompt
Think about the virtue of justice. Choose one moment from this unit where justice was present OR where justice was missing. Explain what happened and why you think it was just or unjust. What can we learn from this moment?
✓ Model Answer
"I think justice was missing during the antebellum period when enslaved people were forced to work without pay or freedom. For example, enslaved families could be separated and sold at any time, which is deeply unjust. Justice would have meant recognizing every person's right to freedom and keeping families together. We can learn that when a society allows injustice to continue, it harms everyone — and that standing up for justice, even when it is hard, is one of the most important things people can do."
📝 Scoring Note
Accept any moment from the unit (antebellum slavery, secession, Reconstruction gains, rollback of rights). Look for: (1) A specific moment identified, (2) Connection to justice (present or absent), (3) A lesson or takeaway. Full credit = all three. Partial = moment identified but connection to justice is vague.
