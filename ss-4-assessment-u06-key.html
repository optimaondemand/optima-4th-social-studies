Multiple Choice — Knowledge & Recall
1Who led the first known European expedition to Florida in 1513?
  • A) Christopher Columbus
  • B) Pedro Menéndez de Avilés
  • C) Juan Ponce de León
  • D) Hernando de Soto
✓ Correct Answer
C — Juan Ponce de León
📝 Scoring Note
Students should recall Ponce de León's 1513 expedition as the first documented European contact with Florida.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may confuse Ponce de León with Columbus, who never reached Florida. Students who choose B may be mixing up the founding of St. Augustine (1565, Menéndez) with the first expedition. Students who choose D may confuse Ponce de León with de Soto, who explored Florida's interior later.
2Why was St. Augustine important in colonial Florida history?
  • A) It was the first English settlement in America
  • B) It was the oldest continuously occupied European settlement in the U.S., founded in 1565
  • C) It was built by the Calusa people
  • D) It was a French trading post
✓ Correct Answer
B — It was the oldest continuously occupied European settlement in the U.S., founded in 1565
📝 Scoring Note
Key fact: St. Augustine predates Jamestown (1607) by over 40 years.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may confuse St. Augustine with Jamestown. Students who choose D may confuse Spanish and French colonial efforts in Florida (Fort Caroline was the French settlement).
3What was the main purpose of Spanish missions in Florida?
  • A) To mine for gold
  • B) To convert Native peoples to Christianity and make them loyal to Spain
  • C) To build schools for Spanish children
  • D) To create national parks
✓ Correct Answer
B — To convert Native peoples to Christianity and make them loyal to Spain
📝 Scoring Note
Missions served a dual religious and political purpose — both conversion and territorial control.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may be conflating early explorer motives (gold) with later mission goals. Students who choose D are applying modern concepts to a historical context.
4What material was the Castillo de San Marcos built from, and why was it special?
  • A) Brick — it was easy to find
  • B) Wood — it was cheap
  • C) Coquina — a shell-based stone that absorbed cannonball impacts
  • D) Marble — it was beautiful
✓ Correct Answer
C — Coquina — a shell-based stone that absorbed cannonball impacts
📝 Scoring Note
Students should connect the material (coquina) to its unique defensive property (absorbing rather than shattering).
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose B may recall that earlier forts were wooden (and burned), not understanding that coquina replaced wood specifically because wood was vulnerable. Students who choose A or D may not recall the unique local material.
5What happened to many Timucua and Apalachee people during the mission period?
  • A) They grew wealthier and gained more land
  • B) Many died from European diseases, faced forced labor, and were displaced from their homelands
  • C) They moved to Spain voluntarily
  • D) Nothing changed for them
✓ Correct Answer
B — Many died from European diseases, faced forced labor, and were displaced from their homelands
📝 Scoring Note
This is a sensitive topic. Look for accurate understanding of the devastating impacts of colonization on Native peoples.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A or D may hold misconceptions that colonization was beneficial or neutral for Native peoples. This should be addressed with care and historical evidence.
6What was daily life like for Native peoples living at Spanish missions?
  • A) They lived freely with no rules
  • B) They worked in fields, attended church services, and followed Spanish schedules and rules
  • C) They only played games
  • D) They traded with the French
✓ Correct Answer
B — They worked in fields, attended church services, and followed Spanish schedules and rules
📝 Scoring Note
Students should understand that mission life was structured and controlled by the Spanish, not voluntary or recreational.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may romanticize mission life. The reality was that Native peoples had little autonomy and were required to follow Spanish religious and labor practices.
7Which of the following is true about Mission San Luis?
  • A) It was a British fort
  • B) It was an Apalachee and Spanish mission community in present-day Tallahassee
  • C) It was built in the 1800s
  • D) It was only used for farming
✓ Correct Answer
B — It was an Apalachee and Spanish mission community in present-day Tallahassee
📝 Scoring Note
Mission San Luis is a key example of Spanish-Native coexistence (though under Spanish control). Students should recall its location and dual community nature.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may confuse Spanish missions with British forts from later periods. Students who choose C may have difficulty placing colonial-era events in the correct century (Mission San Luis was active in the late 1600s).
8How can architecture and artifacts help historians understand the past?
  • A) They cannot — only written documents are useful
  • B) They provide physical evidence of how people lived, what they built, and what they valued
  • C) They only show what buildings looked like
  • D) They are too old to be useful
✓ Correct Answer
B — They provide physical evidence of how people lived, what they built, and what they valued
📝 Scoring Note
This question assesses historical thinking skills — understanding that material culture is a valid and important type of historical evidence.
⚠ Misconception Watch
Students who choose A may believe only written records count as history. Students who choose C have a narrow view of what architecture reveals — it tells us about values, priorities, and daily life, not just appearance.
B
Section B
Source Task — The Castillo de San Marcos
🏰
Historical Source — The Castillo de San Marcos
"The fort was built using coquina, a local stone made from compressed shells. Its walls were over 12 feet thick. When enemy cannonballs hit the walls, the soft coquina absorbed the impact instead of shattering. The fort protected St. Augustine for over 300 years and still stands today."
1Why did the Spanish choose coquina as a building material? Use evidence from the source.
✓ Model Answer
"The Spanish chose coquina because it was a local stone that absorbed cannonball impacts instead of shattering. The source says the walls were over 12 feet thick, making the fort very strong."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for at least one specific detail from the source (coquina absorbing impacts, 12-foot walls, local material) and an explanation of why that mattered. Full credit = specific evidence + explanation. Partial credit = evidence only or explanation only.
2What does the Castillo tell us about what was important to the Spanish in colonial Florida?
✓ Model Answer
"The Castillo shows that protecting St. Augustine was very important to the Spanish. They invested decades building a strong fort, which tells us defense and holding their territory mattered greatly."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for an inference about Spanish priorities (defense, territory, protecting their settlement). Full credit = clear inference connected to evidence. Partial = vague statement without connection to the fort.
3How is the Castillo de San Marcos an example of using architecture as a historical source?
✓ Model Answer
"The Castillo is a historical source because by studying its materials, design, and location, historians can learn about Spanish military priorities, local resources, and how long Spain controlled this area."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for understanding that physical structures are evidence, not just buildings. Students should explain what specific aspects of the Castillo (materials, design, location) reveal about the past. Full credit = names specific aspects + explains what they reveal. Partial = general statement about buildings as sources.

Section C
Socratic Response — Explanation & Reasoning
✍The Spanish built missions to convert Native peoples and expand Spain's power. But missions also caused great harm — disease, forced labor, and the loss of Native cultures and lives. In your own words, explain both the Spanish goals AND the effects on Native peoples. Why is it important to understand both sides?
✓ Model Answer
"The Spanish wanted to spread Christianity and expand their empire. Missions helped them control Native peoples and claim more land. But for the Timucua and Apalachee, missions brought suffering — diseases killed many, they were forced to work and give up their traditions, and many were displaced. It is important to understand both sides because history is not one-sided. If we only tell the Spanish story, we miss the real human cost of colonization."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for: (1) Explanation of Spanish goals (conversion, territorial expansion, control), (2) Explanation of effects on Native peoples (disease, forced labor, cultural loss, displacement), (3) Reasoning about why both perspectives matter. Full credit = all three elements. Partial = one or two elements present.
✍Imagine you are a historian studying Mission San Luis. What kinds of evidence would you look for to understand daily life at the mission? Name at least two types of evidence and explain what each might tell you.
✓ Model Answer
"I would look for artifacts like pottery, tools, and food remains — these would tell me what people ate and how they worked. I would also look at the layout of buildings to understand how space was organized and who lived where. Written records from Spanish priests could tell me about daily schedules and rules."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for: (1) At least two types of evidence named (artifacts, architecture/buildings, written records, oral histories, etc.), (2) Explanation of what each type reveals. Full credit = 2+ types with clear explanations. Partial = types named but explanations missing, or only one type with explanation.

Section D
Virtue Lens — Justice Reflection
⚖ Virtue Prompt — Justice
Think about the concept of justice. Was the way Spanish colonizers treated Native peoples just or unjust? Choose one specific example from this unit and explain your thinking. What would justice have looked like in that situation?
✓ Model Answer
"I think the treatment of Native peoples at missions was unjust. For example, the Apalachee at Mission San Luis were forced to work in fields and attend church even though they had their own beliefs and traditions. Justice would have meant respecting their right to live freely and choose their own way of life instead of forcing Spanish rules on them."
📝 Scoring Note
Look for: (1) A clear position (just or unjust), (2) A specific example from the unit, (3) An explanation of what justice would have looked like. Full credit = all three. Partial = position stated but example is vague or no alternative vision of justice provided.
