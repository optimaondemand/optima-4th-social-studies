Knowledge Check
⏱️ ~10 min
1What does a historian study?
  • A) Only famous people from long ago
  • B) People, places, and events from the past
  • C) Only wars and battles
  • D) Things that will happen in the future
2Which of these is a primary source?
  • A) A textbook about the Seminole Wars
  • B) A Wikipedia article about Florida
  • C) A letter written by a soldier during the Seminole Wars
  • D) A documentary film made in 2020
3Which of these is a secondary source?
  • A) A diary entry from 1845
  • B) A textbook chapter about Florida's history
  • C) An old photograph from 1900
  • D) A handwritten map drawn by an explorer
4What is the difference between print and electronic media?
  • A) Print sources are physical (books, newspapers); electronic sources are digital (websites, databases)
  • B) Print sources are always older than electronic sources
  • C) Electronic sources are always more accurate than print sources
  • D) There is no real difference between them
5What does "chronology" mean?
  • A) The study of ancient clocks
  • B) A type of history book
  • C) A list of important people
  • D) The order in which events happened over time
6The word root "arch-" means:
  • A) Important or powerful
  • B) Ancient or beginning
  • C) To build or construct
  • D) To write or record
7What is an artifact?
  • A) A type of history book
  • B) A modern tool used by scientists
  • C) An object made or used by people in the past
  • D) A story passed down through families
8What does "evidence" mean for historians?
  • A) Information that helps prove or support a claim
  • B) A guess about what happened
  • C) A story someone tells about the past
  • D) A picture drawn by an artist
B
Section B
Source & Map Task
⏱️ ~12 min
📄
Source Scenario

A historian finds an old photograph of a Florida town from 1920. Next to it is a newspaper article from 2010 describing the same town.

Use what you know about primary and secondary sources to answer the questions below.
9Which source is primary and which is secondary? Explain how you know.
Q9: Identify each source type and explain your reasoning.
10What kind of evidence could the photograph give that the newspaper article cannot?
Q10: Describe unique evidence from the photograph.
11If you wanted to put these sources in chronological order, which comes first and why?
Q11: Put the sources in chronological order and explain.
C
Section C
Socratic Response
⏱️ ~15 min
🤔

Think deeply! Use what you learned about sources and evidence to write your best answers.

12Imagine you found an old letter written by a Florida farmer in 1850 and a textbook chapter about Florida farming. Compare these two sources. Which would tell you more about what daily life was really like? Use at least two pieces of evidence from what you learned about primary and secondary sources.
Q12: Compare the two sources using evidence.
13Why is it important for historians to put events in chronological order? Give an example of how mixing up the order of events could change the story.
Q13: Explain the importance of chronological order with an example.
D
Section D
Virtue Lens Reflection
⏱️ ~5 min
⭐ Unit Virtue — Honesty
Honesty means telling the truth and being fair, even when it is difficult. Historians must be honest about the evidence they find.
14The virtue for this unit is honesty. Why is honesty important for a historian? What could happen if a historian was not honest about the evidence they found?
Q14: Reflect on honesty and historical evidence.
