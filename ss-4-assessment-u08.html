Source Study: Florida Before the Civil War
Read the source excerpt and study the map below. Then answer the two evidence questions using details from the source.

📜
Historical Source — Florida in 1860

Type: Historical Summary  |  Period: Antebellum Florida, c. 1860

"By 1860, Florida had about 140,000 people. Nearly half — about 62,000 — were enslaved African Americans. They worked on plantations growing cotton, sugar, and tobacco. Enslaved people had no freedom, no pay, and no legal rights. Families could be separated and sold at any time. Despite these conditions, enslaved people built communities, maintained cultural traditions, and resisted their bondage in many ways."

Socratic Response
⏱️ ~15 Min
🧠

Think about everything you have learned about Florida's path to statehood, the Civil War, and Reconstruction. These are big, important questions — take your time and share your best thinking.

⭐

Strong responses include: Clear explanations, specific examples or facts from what you learned, and your own thoughtful reasoning.

Socratic Response 1: Florida seceded from the Union to protect slavery. After the war, Reconstruction brought new rights — but those rights were later rolled back. In your own words, explain: What changed during Reconstruction, and what happened when Reconstruction ended? Why does this pattern matter?
Socratic Response 2: Think about what it means to be a state versus a territory. When Florida became a state in 1845, some people celebrated — but statehood also meant that slavery was protected by state law. Explain how statehood could mean different things for different groups of people in Florida.
